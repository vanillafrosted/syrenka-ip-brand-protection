**Effective Date**: July 5, 2026

## Overview

These are the official editorial guidelines for the Syrenka English website. All writers, editors, and contributors must follow these principles.

## Key Principles

### 1. Narrator Identity

- **The narrator is always Ray Kono**
- Maintain the same voice and perspective throughout the entire website
- This consistency will carry through to future Kindle books
- **Ray Kono is the narrative voice; the real experiences belong to Maki.** All events, facts, and emotions must remain true to Maki's actual experience — Ray Kono is simply the consistent name and voice used to tell that real story across the website.
- Do not invent experiences or emotions to make the story more dramatic. When in doubt, prioritize authenticity over dramatic effect.

### 2. Website Purpose

- This is **a blog sharing real experiences of protecting a small brand in Japan**
- This is **NOT** a legal advice website
- Content should be personal, authentic, and experience-focused

### 3. Translation Philosophy

English articles are **NOT literal translations** of Japanese articles.

Each article should be:
- **Recreated as a natural English article** for international readers
- **Preserve the original experience** and narrative
- **Adapt tone and pacing** for English-speaking audiences
- **Maintain fidelity to the source** Japanese article

### 4. Cultural Context & Explanations

Whenever Japanese-specific systems or culture appear, **explain them for international readers**.

This is one of the unique strengths of the English website.

**Key topics to explain:**
- Japanese copyright system
- Trademark registration process
- Japan Patent Office (JPO)
- Mints (Japanese intellectual property term)
- 内容証明郵便 (Naiyo Shomei / Certified mail)
- Japanese e-commerce platforms (Amazon Japan, Rakuten, Mercari, Qoo10)
- Japanese court procedures
- Japanese business culture norms
- Customer communication customs in Japan
- Paper-based court filing systems

**Example approach:**
- Rather than: "I filed a naiyo shomei"
- Write: "I filed certified mail (naiyo shomei), which is Japan's official method for documenting important business communications"

### 5. Content Integrity

- **Never invent experiences or events**
- All experiences must remain faithful to the original Japanese articles
- When in doubt, verify against the Japanese source

### 6. Prefer Authentic Small Details Over Generalized Storytelling

- The most valuable parts of a story are often its smallest, most specific details (e.g., discovering a copied listing on a phone after a morning jog, rather than a generic "at my laptop" scene)
- Always ask the source (Maki) for concrete specifics before defaulting to generic dramatization
- These small, real moments are what make the articles feel authentic and distinguish Ray Kono's voice from generic brand-protection content

## Article Structure: Required vs. Optional Sections

### Principle: Narrative Quality Over Template Completeness

The Prototype Article Template is our structural guide, but not every article requires every section. **Narrative quality and reader experience always take priority over template completeness.**

### Required Sections (All Articles)

Every article MUST include:

1. **SEO Metadata** — Title, description, og tags, canonical URL
2. **Main Narrative** — The core story and experience
3. **Key Takeaways** — Lessons Ray learned through her experience
4. **Practical Tips** — Actionable steps readers can take
5. **Disclaimer** — Legal disclaimer about limitations and scope
6. **Call to Action** — Encouragement for readers to take next steps

### Optional Sections (Include if Natural to the Article)

These sections enhance certain articles but should only be included if they:
- Feel natural to the narrative flow
- Genuinely help the reader
- Do not interrupt the story or feel promotional

Optional components include:

- **Kindle Recommendation** — Cross-promotion to Kindle books (only if it flows naturally from the article's content)
- **FAQ** — Common questions and answers (only if article naturally raises questions)
- **Supplemental Notes** — Additional context or examples (only if needed for clarity)
- **Promotional/Navigational Components** — Related articles, series links, or promotional callouts (only if they enhance navigation without disrupting narrative)

### Decision Guidelines

**Include an optional section if:**
- It answers questions the article naturally raises
- It flows naturally from the narrative
- It adds genuine value rather than feeling like a sales pitch
- It doesn't interrupt the reader's experience

**Omit an optional section if:**
- It feels promotional or forced
- It breaks narrative continuity
- The article tells a complete story without it
- Including it would distract from Ray's core experience
