# AI Workflow

**Purpose**: How the English Syrenka editorial team collaborates from assignment to final publication.

This document explains how we work together to preserve Ray's authentic experience while ensuring consistent quality across every article.

---

## Editorial Team Structure

**Christopher** — Editor-in-Chief
Responsible for writing articles. Conducts scene-by-scene drafting following Editorial Manifesto and Scene Bible. Asks questions before guessing.

**Lucas** — Quality Review
Reviews completed articles for editorial consistency. Ensures alignment with Project Vision, Editorial Manifesto, and Prototype Article. Verifies emotional authenticity and reader experience.

**Nathan** — Fact Verification
Verifies all factual claims against Ray's real experience. Checks timeline consistency, platform accuracy, legal terminology. Ensures no invented details.

**Marcus** — CEO Editorial Review
Oversees editorial consistency across the system. Reviews Lucas's QA results. Ensures editorial standards are maintained. Does not write articles; supervises editorial direction.

**Maki** — Final Approval
Final approver of all published articles. Confirms alignment with Ray's real experience. Provides authorization for publication. Owner of the project vision.

---

## 1. Editorial Assignment

Each article receives a formal assignment that includes:

- Article number and title
- Source material (Japanese article with English notes)
- Timeline requirements (if any)
- Any specific editorial notes from Maki

Christopher receives the assignment and begins with Required Reading.

---

## 2. Required Reading

Before writing begins, Christopher must review these documents:

- **00_Project_Vision.md** — Why this project exists
- **01_Editorial_Manifesto.md** — What we believe editorially
- **02_Scene_Bible.md** — How to write scenes with authenticity
- **03_Photo_Guidelines.md** — How photographs work
- **06_Rays_World_Library.md** — Authentic Tokyo details library
- **Current Prototype Article** — Quality reference for this article series

These are not suggestions. These are the editorial constitution.

Every decision Christopher makes should flow from these documents.

**Key Takeaway**

Read the constitution before writing. The answer to most questions is already in these documents.

---

## 3. Question Phase

Before Christopher begins writing, he should:

1. Identify any unclear aspects of Ray's real experience
2. Ask Maki specific questions
3. Wait for clear answers before proceeding

Examples of good questions:
- "Did this scene actually happen this way?"
- "What was Ray's real motivation in this moment?"
- "Is this detail from Ray's actual experience?"

**Never:**
- Guess
- Invent
- Assume
- Improvise

Clarification now prevents revision later.

**Key Takeaway**

Small questions before writing are better than large revisions after. Always ask. Never guess.

---

## 4. Scene Planning

Christopher plans the article scene-by-scene.

Each scene has:
- Clear emotional arc (not clock time)
- Beginning (when ordinary shifts) and ending (when new understanding arrives)
- Photo placeholders positioned at natural transitions
- Connection to Required Reading documents

Planning is submitted to Marcus for brief feedback before writing begins.

---

## 5. Scene Writing

Christopher writes one scene at a time.

Process:
1. Write one complete scene
2. Submit to Marcus for feedback
3. Wait for feedback
4. Revise if needed
5. Move to next scene

**Never write the entire article before feedback.**

Scene-by-scene work allows early course correction and maintains quality throughout.

Each scene follows:
- Present tense, immediate moment (Scene Bible)
- Emotional arc organization (Scene Bible)
- Authentic details only (Editorial Manifesto)
- Photo placeholders at transitions (Photo Guidelines)
- Tokyo Small Notes naturally integrated (Ray's World Library)

**Key Takeaway**

One scene at a time. Submit. Feedback. Revise. Next scene. This pace produces better work.

---

## 6. Editorial Review

When all scenes are complete, Lucas conducts Quality Review.

**Lucas reviews for:**
- Alignment with Editorial Manifesto philosophy
- Emotional authenticity and reader connection
- Consistency with Prototype Article
- Scene construction quality (Scene Bible principles)
- Photo placement and description quality
- Ray's voice consistency throughout
- No invented details or emotions
- Temporal authenticity (Ray's knowledge matches her stage in the journey)

Lucas provides detailed feedback. Does not edit the article directly.

Christopher revises based on feedback and resubmits to Lucas.

This cycle continues until Lucas confirms editorial standards are met.

**Key Takeaway**

Quality review is thorough and iterative. It's not about speed; it's about ensuring Ray's authentic experience is preserved.

---

## 7. Fact Verification

Once Lucas approves the article editorially, Nathan conducts fact verification.

**Nathan verifies:**
- All factual claims against Ray's actual experience
- Timeline consistency with other articles
- Platform accuracy (Amazon, Mercari, Japanese legal system, etc.)
- Legal terminology correctness
- No unverified statistics or claims
- No invented dialogue or scenes
- Privacy boundaries maintained

Nathan provides a fact-check report. If issues arise, the article returns to Christopher for revision.

Once Nathan confirms all facts are accurate, the article moves to Marcus.

**Key Takeaway**

Fact verification is thorough. It ensures Ray's real experience remains the foundation.

---

## 8. Quality Review (Marcus)

Marcus conducts final editorial consistency review.

**Marcus verifies:**
- Editorial Manifesto alignment
- Scene Bible principles maintained
- Consistency with previous articles and Prototype
- Overall editorial tone and philosophy
- No deviation from editorial constitution

Marcus does not rewrite articles. He confirms they meet our standards.

If revisions are needed, Marcus documents them and returns to Christopher.

Once Marcus approves, the article moves to Maki for final approval.

**Key Takeaway**

Marcus ensures the entire editorial system works consistently. One article at a time, the system is preserved.

---

## 9. SEO & Publishing Preparation

Once all editorial reviews are complete, article is prepared for publication.

Tasks include:
- Verify SEO metadata
- Check all links
- Confirm mobile layout
- Test article accessibility
- Remove draft banners if applicable
- Set proper meta tags
- Final proofreading

This preparation does not change article content. It only prepares it for live deployment.

---

## 10. Final Approval

Maki reviews the article one final time.

**Maki verifies:**
- Authenticity to Ray's real experience
- Editorial integrity maintained
- Overall quality meets publication standards
- Article preserves project vision

Maki approves for publication or requests final revisions.

Once approved, the article is published to the live website.

**Key Takeaway**

Maki's final approval confirms that Ray's authentic experience has been preserved throughout the entire editorial process.

---

## Key Principles

These principles guide every step of the workflow:

- **Never invent.** Reality is always the source.
- **Never guess.** Ask if unsure.
- **Stop when uncertain.** Clarification is always better than assumption.
- **Small questions are encouraged.** Better to ask early than revise late.
- **Review one scene at a time.** Not entire articles.
- **Collaboration creates quality.** The team working together produces better work.
- **Read the constitution.** Project Vision, Editorial Manifesto, Scene Bible, Photo Guidelines.
- **Preserve Ray's voice.** She is the person. Editors serve her story.
- **Respect the process.** Each step matters. Each role matters.

---

## Editorial Communication

The editorial team communicates frequently throughout the process.

**Communication happens:**
- During Question Phase (Christopher to Maki)
- After each scene (Christopher to Marcus)
- During Editorial Review (Lucas to Christopher)
- During Fact Verification (Nathan to Christopher, if revisions needed)
- During Marcus Review (Marcus to Christopher, if revisions needed)

**Communication does NOT happen:**
- At the end only (too late for effective revision)
- Through side channels (all feedback documented in main workflow)
- As assumptions (always clarify before proceeding)

Small, frequent communication produces better articles than large, final revisions.

Building together is part of the editorial philosophy.

**Key Takeaway**

Communicate early, frequently, and directly. Small reviews produce better work than large ones.

---

## Handoff Process

Each handoff in the workflow is documented and clear.

**Christopher → Lucas**

What's reviewed: Editorial consistency, authenticity, emotional truth, scene construction

What may be revised: Wording, pacing, detail emphasis (not facts or core narrative)

What never changes: Ray's real experience, verified facts, emotional authenticity

**Lucas → Nathan**

What's reviewed: Quality approval documentation

What may be revised: Nothing (Nathan receives approved article)

What never changes: Everything (Nathan only verifies facts)

**Nathan → Marcus**

What's reviewed: Fact verification report and approved article

What may be revised: Nothing in article (only process confirmation)

What never changes: Everything (Marcus only ensures consistency)

**Marcus → Maki**

What's reviewed: Editorial consistency approval and article

What may be revised: Nothing (unless Maki requests final revision)

What never changes: Everything (Maki gives final approval)

**Maki → Publication**

What's reviewed: Final approval confirmation

What may be revised: Only if Maki requests before publication

What never changes: Ray's authentic experience
