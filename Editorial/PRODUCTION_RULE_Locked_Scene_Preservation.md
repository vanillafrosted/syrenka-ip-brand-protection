# Production Rule: Locked Scene Preservation

**Established**: 2026-07-10
**Approved By**: Maki (Project Owner)
**Applies To**: All 83 English Syrenka Articles
**Status**: Permanent Production Workflow Rule

---

## Purpose

Protect LOCKED scenes from accidental loss, mixing with older drafts, or future editing.

Maintain complete editorial history and increase confidence during long multi-scene projects.

---

## Definition: LOCKED Scene

A scene is LOCKED when it has completed the full workflow:

1. ✓ Writing (scene content completed)
2. ✓ Editorial Review (Stephen Mitchell approval)
3. ✓ Fact Verification (Nathan Morgan verification)
4. ✓ CEO Review (Marcus Reynolds consistency check)
5. ✓ Final Approval (Maki authorization)

Once LOCKED: Scene becomes permanent, immutable part of production record.

---

## The Rule: Five-Step Preservation Process

### Step 1: Save LOCKED Scene Immediately
After final approval, save the LOCKED scene content immediately to:
- Article's working document (Google Docs or designated system)
- Marked with: **[LOCKED - SCENE X] - [DATE]**
- Include all approved text exactly as finalized

### Step 2: Mark as LOCKED in Production Record
Document the lock status:
- Scene number and title
- Date locked
- All reviewers who approved
- Link to final approved version

### Step 3: Never Replace or Overwrite LOCKED Scene
**LOCKED scenes are permanent.**
- Do not edit LOCKED scenes
- Do not revise LOCKED scenes
- Do not replace LOCKED scenes with new versions
- Do not merge LOCKED scenes with drafts

### Step 4: Continue Writing From Latest LOCKED Version
When writing the next scene:
- Read the latest LOCKED version of previous scene
- Ensure scene boundaries are clear and preserved
- Begin new scene only after confirming LOCKED version
- Never write from an outdated or draft version

### Step 5: Assemble Production Article From LOCKED Scenes Only
When article is complete:
- Collect ALL LOCKED scene versions
- Assemble in chronological order
- Verify each scene is marked [LOCKED]
- Create final article from these approved versions only
- No drafts, no variations, no experimental versions

---

## Why This Matters

**Prevent Accidental Loss**
- LOCKED scenes are safe from being accidentally overwritten
- Complete editorial history preserved
- Recovery is always possible

**Prevent Draft Mixing**
- No confusion between old drafts and approved versions
- No accidental rollback to earlier iterations
- Clear audit trail of what was approved

**Preserve Quality**
- All approved editorial decisions are protected
- Writer cannot inadvertently undo approved work
- Each scene's integrity maintained

**Simplify Assembly**
- Production Article assembled from clearly marked sources
- No ambiguity about which version to use
- Final article is provably complete and approved

**Increase Confidence**
- Team knows LOCKED scenes are protected
- Long projects (Scenes 1-9+) don't accumulate draft risk
- Peace of mind during extended writing phases

---

## Exception Handling

**If a LOCKED scene has a factual error discovered:**

1. Do NOT overwrite the LOCKED scene
2. Report error to Maki immediately
3. Create new version marked: **[LOCKED REVISION 1]**
4. Go through full approval workflow again
5. When approved, mark new version as LOCKED REVISION
6. Maintain both versions in record (original + revision)
7. Final article uses LOCKED REVISION version

**All error corrections go through same full approval workflow.**
