## TEMPLATE STRUCTURE & GUIDELINES

### 1. SEO TITLE
**Maximum**: 60 characters (including spaces)
**Format**: [Action/Story] + [Japan Context] + [Benefit]

**Purpose**:
- Appears in Google search results
- Must clearly communicate article value
- Include primary keyword naturally

**Example**:
- ❌ "How I Protected My Brand"
- ✅ "How I Stopped Counterfeit Sales on Amazon Japan"

**Guidelines**:
- Lead with the problem or action
- Include geographic or platform context
- Avoid clickbait
- Be specific and descriptive

---

### 2. ARTICLE METADATA (SEO)
**Format**: YAML or structured data

```
Title: [SEO Title]
Description: [155-160 characters explaining article value]
Author: Ray Kono
Date: [Publication Date]
Updated: [If updated]
Category: [Brand Protection / Trademark / Copyright / E-Commerce / Legal]
Tags: [3-5 relevant tags]
Slug: [URL-friendly version of title]
```

**Purpose**:
- Provides search engine information
- Enables internal site organization
- Tracks publication metadata

**Example**:
```
Title: How I Stopped Counterfeit Sales on Amazon Japan
Description: Ray Kono shares her real experience discovering and removing counterfeit products from Amazon Japan, including what worked and what didn't.
Category: Brand Protection
Tags: Amazon Japan, Counterfeits, E-Commerce, Brand Protection, Enforcement
```

### 3. HERO IMAGE
**Dimensions**: 1200px × 600px (or 16:9 ratio)
**File Format**: WebP or optimized JPEG
**File Size**: Under 100KB

**Purpose**:
- Creates visual interest
- Appears in social sharing
- Improves page aesthetics
- Breaks up text on mobile

**Guidelines**:
- Must reflect article content
- Should include Syrenka branding or logo subtly
- Avoid generic stock photos
- Use real photos when possible
- Include alt text for accessibility
- Maintain consistent visual style across website

**Alt Text Example**:
- "Amazon Japan product listing showing counterfeit Syrenka product"

### 4. INTRODUCTION
**Recommended Length**: 100-150 words
**Tone**: Conversational, personal, engaging

**Subsection A: Hook**
Start with a relevant question or situation that readers relate to.

**Example**:
"Have you ever searched for your own product online and found it listed by someone else? That's exactly what happened to me on Amazon Japan."

**Purpose**: Capture attention immediately

---

**Subsection B: What Readers Will Learn**
Clearly state the value proposition (2-3 sentences).

**Example**:
"In this article, I'll share exactly what I discovered, the steps I took to remove the counterfeits, which tactics actually worked, and what I would do differently next time. If you sell on Amazon Japan or other Japanese e-commerce platforms, this experience will help you protect your brand."

**Purpose**:
- Sets reader expectations
- Improves SEO (keywords)
- Encourages readers to continue

---

**Subsection C: Why This Topic Matters**
Briefly explain the broader context (1-2 sentences).

**Example**:
"Counterfeit products damage brand reputation and customer trust. For small sellers in Japan, protecting your brand is often a DIY effort—there's no legal team to handle it. Learning from real experiences is often more valuable than generic advice."

**Purpose**:
- Establishes authority
- Contextualizes the challenge
- Motivates reader engagement

### 5. MAIN ARTICLE
**Recommended Length**: 1,200-2,000 words
**Tone**: Ray Kono's authentic voice—experienced, practical, honest
**Perspective**: Written entirely from Ray Kono's first-person perspective

**Structure**:
Use natural storytelling with clear sections. The article flows chronologically through Ray's experience.

**Core Guidelines**:
- **Paragraph Length**: 2-4 sentences maximum (easier to read on mobile)
- **Subheadings**: Use H3 (###) every 200-300 words to break up content
- **Real Details**: Include specific dates, numbers, platforms, names (when appropriate)
- **Honesty**: Share what didn't work as well as what did
- **Voice**: Write as if explaining to a business owner friend (not a lawyer)
- **First-Person Narrative**: "I discovered...", "I tried...", "I learned..."

**Japan Context Integration (Critical)**:
When Japanese systems, platforms, or business culture appear, explain them naturally within the story flow. Do NOT create a separate "Japan Context" section. Instead:

- Explain Amazon Japan's reporting process as you describe using it: "When I logged into Amazon Seller Central—Japan's version works slightly differently than the US site—I found the counterfeit listing..."
- Explain mints, naiyo shomei, or business customs as they become relevant to your experience: "I decided to send certified mail (naiyo shomei), which is Japan's official method for documenting business communications..."
- Integrate platform differences naturally: "Unlike Amazon.com, Amazon Japan's response time was longer, around 10-15 business days..."
- Explain business culture as it affects your decision-making: "In Japan, following up in person is expected, so I called their customer support line..."

**Japan Context Topics to Naturally Integrate** (as relevant):
- E-Commerce Platforms: Amazon Japan, Rakuten, Mercari, Qoo10
- Legal Systems: Trademark registration, copyright protection, court procedures
- Business Culture: Customer expectations, communication norms, business relationships
- Official Terms: mints, Naiyo Shomei (certified mail), Japan Patent Office (JPO)
- Industry Practices: How sellers operate, communication standards, quality expectations

**Key Elements to Include**:
- Specific problem encountered (Ray's discovery)
- Initial reaction/emotions (Ray's feeling)
- Steps taken in chronological order (Ray's actions)
- Obstacles encountered (Ray's challenges)
- Solutions discovered (Ray's decisions)
- Results achieved (Ray's outcome)
- Lessons learned (Ray's reflection)

**Example Structure**:
```
### The Discovery

[Paragraph 1-2: How I found the counterfeit products]

[Paragraph 3-4: My initial shock/reaction to discovering this]

### Taking Action

[Paragraph 1-2: The first steps I took]

[Paragraph 3-4: The complications I encountered when trying to report]

### What Actually Worked

[Paragraph 1-2: The successful tactics I discovered]

[Paragraph 3-4: Why these tactics were effective—and how Amazon Japan's process differs from the US]

### What Didn't Work

[Paragraph 1-2: The approaches I tried that failed]

[Paragraph 3-4: What I learned from those failures]
```

**Purpose**:
- Deliver the core value of the article
- Establish Ray Kono as a trusted, experienced voice
- Provide specific, actionable information
- Build emotional connection with readers
- Naturally educate international readers about Japanese systems without disrupting narrative flow

### 6. KEY TAKEAWAYS
**Format**: Bulleted list
**Recommended**: 4-6 items
**Length per item**: 1 sentence maximum

**Purpose**:
- Allows busy readers to scan key points
- Reinforces main article message
- Improves SEO (keyword repetition)
- Works well on social media sharing

**Guidelines**:
- Start each with an action verb (Monitor, Report, Document, etc.)
- Make each item independently understandable
- Order by importance or sequence
- Use clear, simple language

**Example**:
```
## Key Takeaways

• Monitor your products on major Japanese e-commerce platforms monthly
• Document counterfeit listings with screenshots and URLs (keep a folder)
• Report counterfeits immediately through each platform's official process
• Follow up with brands/platform support if no action within 10-15 days
• Keep detailed records of all counterfeit reports and resolutions
```

---

### 7. PRACTICAL TIPS
**Format**: Numbered list or expandable sections
**Recommended**: 3-5 actionable tips
**Length per tip**: 2-3 sentences

**Purpose**:
- Provides immediate, usable value
- Differentiates "learnable experience" from "just a story"
- Gives readers concrete next steps
- Improves article utility

**Guidelines**:
- Each tip must be actionable
- Include specific details (not generic advice)
- Based on Ray's real experience
- Consider different skill levels (beginners through advanced)

**Example**:
```
## Practical Tips

**Tip 1: Set Up a Monitoring System**
Don't rely on memory. Create a simple spreadsheet tracking your product SKUs and check Amazon Japan, Rakuten, and Mercari weekly. Set phone reminders if needed. I check mine every Sunday afternoon—it takes 15 minutes.

**Tip 2: Screenshot Everything**
When you find a counterfeit, take screenshots immediately: the product listing, the seller profile, the pricing, the product description. Platforms sometimes remove listings before you can report them. Screenshots are your proof.

**Tip 3: Use Naiyo Shomei (Certified Mail) for Serious Issues**
For repeat offenders or high-value counterfeits, send official certified mail (naiyo shomei) to the seller's registered address. This creates an official record and shows you're serious. It's surprisingly effective in Japan.
```

**Placement**: Toward end of article

**Purpose**:
- Keeps readers on website (improves metrics)
- Builds content interconnection
- Guides readers through related topics
- Improves SEO through internal linking

**Format**:

## Related Articles

**Guidelines**:
- Links should be genuinely related to article topic
- Use descriptive anchor text (not "click here")
- Include 1-2 sentence description of why readers should read it
- Prioritize earlier articles in Phase 1

**Tone**: Subtle, not promotional

- Maintains narrative continuity

**Guidelines**:
- ❌ Only include if directly relevant to article

### 10. DISCLAIMER
**Length**: 2-3 sentences
**Placement**: After main content, before Related Articles
**Tone**: Professional but conversational

**Purpose**:
- Protects legally (article is experience, not legal advice)
- Sets reader expectations clearly
- Builds trust through transparency
- Distinguishes from legal advice websites

**Standard Disclaimer Template**:
```
## Disclaimer

This article shares Ray Kono's real experience protecting Syrenka's brand in Japan. It is not legal advice. Every brand protection situation is unique, and strategies that worked for Syrenka may not work for your situation. For specific legal questions about trademark, copyright, or brand protection in Japan, please consult with a qualified intellectual property attorney.
```

**Customization Guidelines**:
- Adapt wording if article contains legal-adjacent information
- Never remove or weaken this section
- Maintain consistent wording across all articles

---

### 11. CALL TO ACTION
**Length**: 1-2 sentences
**Placement**: Final section of article
**Tone**: Encouraging, not pushy

**Purpose**:
- Guides reader to next action
- Drives engagement/community building
- Provides exit path from article
- Encourages return visits

**Format Options**:

**Option A: Internal Link**
```
What happened to your brand when you discovered counterfeits? Share your story in the comments—I read every one and it helps other sellers learn from real experiences.
```

**Option B: Newsletter/Updates**
```
Stories like these are shared regularly on the Syrenka blog. Subscribe to get new articles about protecting your brand in Japan.
```

**Option C: Related Topic Direction**
```
Ready for the next step? Read "How to File a Trademark in Japan: A Seller's Guide" to understand how Ray protected Syrenka legally.
```

**Guidelines**:
- Make it easy for readers to know what to do next
- Avoid pushy or salesy language
- Stay true to Ray Kono's authentic voice

- Check that callout boxes don't overflow

- Include alt text for all images
- Use descriptive heading text (not "Section 1")

3. **Showcase Ray Kono's authentic voice**

4. **Provide practical, actionable value**

   - Not too simple (should utilize full template depth)

   - Should show why this blog differs from generic brand protection advice
   - Should highlight the "real experience in Japan" angle
   - Should feel like Ray sharing lessons with a business owner friend

### Recommended Article Type

Based on these criteria, the **first prototype article should be about:**

**A specific real case where Ray discovered counterfeit products on a Japanese e-commerce platform (Amazon Japan, Rakuten, Mercari, or Qoo10) and took action.**

**Why this is ideal:**

✅ **Complete narrative arc**
- Discovery → Investigation → Action taken → Results → Lessons learned
- Perfect for demonstrating the full template

✅ **Natural Japan Context integration**
- Can explain specific platform features (Amazon Japan vs US version) as part of the action narrative
- Can explain reporting procedures unique to Japanese platforms within describing what Ray did
- Can explain marketplace culture differences as she encounters them

✅ **Ray's authentic voice**
- Practical brand protection experience (not theoretical)
- Specific details (seller accounts, counterfeit listing URLs patterns, response times)
- Honest about challenges (what made reporting difficult)
- Professional insights based on 36+ lawsuits

✅ **Actionable for readers**
- Other sellers on Amazon Japan/Rakuten/Mercari can replicate steps
- Practical tips are directly applicable
- Key Takeaways become a monitoring system readers can adopt

✅ **Moderate scope**
- Bounded by a specific incident (manageable word count)
- Natural three-act structure
- Enough detail to be valuable, not overwhelming

✅ **Unique value**
- Generic articles say "report counterfeits to platforms"
- Ray's article shows: actual procedures, response times, follow-up strategies, persistence required
- Demonstrates why it's different from legal advice (it's real seller experience)

### What NOT to Recommend

❌ **Avoid for first prototype:**
- An article that requires extensive legal explanation (too heavy on Japan Context)
- An article about a failed experience with no resolution (incomplete narrative)
- An article about a complex trademark/copyright case (may need excessive legal context)
- A very short article (doesn't utilize full template)
- An article that's primarily opinion/theory (needs to showcase "real experience")
